Aṣa – $28 million

The richest female musician in Africa is Bukola Elemide whose stage name is Aṣa. She is a well-known and lovely Nigerian/French singer, songwriter, performer, and recording artist who always bless audiences with her heartfelt melodies.

Although Aṣa was born in Paris to Nigerian parents, her family moved her to Nigeria after she turned two. There, she spent 18 years in Lagos City before relocating back to Paris, where her career as an artist really took off.

Asa’s music career started in 2007 after releasing her first song “Eyé Adaba” from her first platinum-selling self-titled album, Aṣa. The album charted across Europe, Asia, and Africa. “Jailer” is Asa’s best song yet, although she claims it is not her favorite.

Her unique approach and style of music make her stand out among every other Nigerian artist. More so, she is not so big on collaborations.

Altogether, the talented artist Asa has 5 studio albums, 2 live albums, and 11 singles. With a whopping net worth of $28 million, she is fourth among Nigeria’s richest singers.

2. Oumou Sangaré – $12 million

Oumou Sangaré is the second on the list of the Top 10 Richest Female Musicians In Africa. Early in the 1990s, Sangaré began her musical career. Since then, she has produced six studio albums. She is renowned for having a soulful, commanding voice.

However, her music combines elements of traditional Malian music with soul and funk influences from the West. Sangaré’s estimated net worth as of 2022 is $12 million.

Sangaré has won numerous honors, including a Grammy Award nomination, and her music has received high appreciation from reviewers all around the world.

Moreover, Oumou’s popularity has aided in promoting African music and culture.

Currently, she is one of the most well-known and admired African performers of all time and serves as an inspiration to several musicians and fans worldwide. Also, she owns multiple enterprises in Mali and is one of the richest female artists in Africa. She is a successful businesswoman.

3. Akothee – $10 million

On this list is a Kenyan musician and entrepreneur Esther Akoth popularly known as Akothee. According to Forbes, she has an estimated net worth of $10 million.

She is the creator of the nonprofit Akothee Foundation, the real estate company Akothee Homes, and Akothee Safaris.

One of the wealthiest female musicians in Africa, the Kenyan performer is well-known for her extravagant lifestyle, which includes her love of expensive automobiles and spacious homes. She garnered media attention in 2016 when she spent $220,000 on a Rolls-Royce for herself.

4. Tiwa Savage – $9 million

Tiwa Savage is one of the most popular, respected, and richest female artists in Nigeria currently. She started her career as a backup singer to some world-famous singers in the UK; George Micheal and Mary J. Blige.

In 2012, she relocated to Nigeria to continue her music career journey by joining Mavin Records. Ever since she has produced several music hits and gathered quite a number of fans that just cannot get over her creativity. However, she is no longer under the Mavin Record but is now an artist under her own label.

With an estimated net worth of $9 million, Tiwa is not only one of the richest musicians in Nigeria but also one of the richest musicians in Africa.

5. Mdee – $8 million

Mdee is a musician who also works as a television presenter. In 2009, Mdee released her debut album, “Nguvu Yetu,” launching her musical career.

The hit singles “Sisi,” “Kutoka Kwa Dunia,” and “Ndugu” were produced as a result of the album’s commercial success in Tanzania.

However, “Kwetu,” Mdee’s sophomore album, was released in 2014 and the album’s main track, shot to the top of the charts in every country in East Africa.

She started presenting “The Show” on MTV Africa in 2018. Mdee is a native of the Tanzanian city of Dar es Salaam. She has Swahili ancestry. Mdee’s estimated net worth as of 2022 is $8 million.

6. Angelique Kidjo – $8 million

Among the top 10 richest musicians in Africa is a super talented and award-winning singer, songwriter, and actress Angelique Kidjo from Benin. She is one of the best-known and most successful African musicians of all time. With an estimated net worth of $8 million, Angelique Kidjo is one of the richest female musicians in Africa.

However, she has recorded numerous critically acclaimed albums over the course of her three-decade music career. Also, she has received many honors, including Grammy and Tony awards.

As a supporter of social justice and human rights, Kidjo frequently expresses her opinions on topics like gender equality and climate change.

7. Salma Sky – $6 million

Salma Sky is a well-known singer with an estimated net worth of $6 million. She has worked with musicians including JK and Macky 2.

However, she is best known for the songs “Nshibe,” “Kutamwisha,” and “I Believe.” Salma is renowned for her expressive voice and knack for relating to her audience.

She has received numerous honors, including the Best Female Artist in Zambian Music Award. Salma is an advocate for women’s rights and a role model for young women in Zambia.

The musician loves spending time with her family and friends and watching football in her free time. (soccer). Her estimated net worth of $6 million made her one of the richest musicians in Africa.

8. Zahara – $6 million

Bulelwa Mkutukana popularly known as Zahara is a South African composer, singer, and songwriter born on November 9, 1987

However, she became well-known in 2011 with the release of her number-one single, “Loliwe.” With an estimated net worth of $6 million, Zahara is one of the richest female musicians in Africa. She earned this amount of money from her lucrative music career.

However, she has released a number of commercially successful singles and albums. In addition to her music career, Zahara has dabbled in acting and has made appearances in a few movies and television programs.

She is currently one of the most successful African musicians thanks to the success of all of her endeavors.

9. Becca – $6 million

Rebecca Akosua Acheampomaa Acheampong known by her stage name Becca is a Ghanaian singer, songwriter, and actor from Ghana with a net worth of $6 million.

She came to the limelight after winning the Miss Malaika Ghana beauty contest in 2007.

However, her debut studio album Sugar was released in 2007; it earned her five nominations at the 2008 Ghana Music Awards

Some of Becca’s accolades include:

  • one Kora Award
  • one National Youth Achievers Award
  • four Ghana Music Awards
  • three 4Syte TV Music Video Awards

In 2013, she headlined the annual Girl Talk concert, which started in 2011. Becca was ranked 94 on E.tv Ghana’s 2013 list of the 100 most influential people in Ghana.

3Music Network included Becca on its list of the Top 30 Most Influential Women in Music in March 2021. Becca has worked with artists such as Hugh Masekela and MI Abaga.

10. Khadja Nin – $5 million

Rounding off the list of the top 10 richest female musicians in Africa is a Congolese actress, singer, and songwriter Khadja Nin. With an estimated net worth of $5 million, she is one of the wealthiest female musicians in Africa.

Khadja Nin started her music career in the early 1980s after being born in the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Her music is renowned for its unique blend of African, Latin American, and European influences and for her deep voice. Throughout her career, Nin has put out numerous albums and received numerous honors, including a Grammy nomination.
