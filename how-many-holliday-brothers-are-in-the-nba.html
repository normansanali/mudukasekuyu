How many Holliday brothers are in the NBA?

 Posted on July 14, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  642 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The Holiday brothers, Justin, Jrue, and Aaron, have carved a unique legacy in the NBA as the only trio of siblings currently competing in the league.

Their journey from childhood to professional basketball has been marked by dedication, talent, and a shared love for the sport.

This Fan Arch feature delves into the remarkable careers and experiences of the Holiday brothers, highlighting their individual achievements and the impact they have collectively made on the basketball scene.

Justin Holiday

Justin Holiday, the oldest of the Holiday brothers, embarked on his professional basketball career in 2011 after going undrafted.

He made his NBA debut with the Philadelphia 76ers and has since played for several teams, including the Golden State Warriors, Atlanta Hawks, Chicago Bulls, New York Knicks, Memphis Grizzlies, Indiana Pacers, Sacramento Kings, and Dallas Mavericks.

Currently, Justin is a part of the Denver Nuggets roster, where he continues to contribute his skills and experience to the team.

Jrue Holiday, known for his elite defensive prowess, has established himself as a standout player in the NBA. Initially drafted 17th overall by the Philadelphia 76ers in 2009, Jrue has had a stellar career, showcasing his defensive abilities and strategic gameplay.

He has played for the 76ers, New Orleans Pelicans, Milwaukee Bucks, and currently, he is a key player for the Boston Celtics.

Jrue's impact transcends his on-court performance, as evidenced by his three-time recognition as the NBA Teammate of the Year in 2020, 2022, and 2023, underscoring his commitment to teamwork and leadership.

Aaron Holiday

The youngest among the Holiday brothers, Aaron Holiday, has been making his mark in the NBA with his skillful play as a scoring point guard.

His journey began at UCLA, where he displayed his basketball prowess and was eventually selected by the Indiana Pacers with the 23rd overall pick in the 2018 NBA Draft.

Aaron has showcased his abilities while playing for the Pacers, Washington Wizards, Atlanta Hawks, and currently, he is a part of the Houston Rockets roster. His journeyman role player career has seen him don the jerseys of multiple teams, contributing his talent and versatility to each one.

Sibling Legacy and Achievements

The Holiday family's basketball legacy is truly remarkable, with all three brothers making significant contributions to the NBA.

While Jrue stands out as a two-time All-Star and a renowned defender, both Justin and Aaron have proven themselves as valuable contributors and key role players for their respective teams.

Despite not being All-Star players, their collective impact on the court and their ability to play alongside each other in the league has solidified their place in NBA history.

Historic Moments

One of the most significant milestones in the Holiday brothers' careers was in 2019 when Justin, Jrue, and Aaron became the first sibling trio to play in the same NBA game.

This historic moment, where the brothers shared the court, exemplified their shared passion for basketball and the bond they have developed over the years.

Their desire to play together on the same team in the future reflects their strong familial connection and their aspirations to make an even greater impact as an official unit in the NBA.

 

The Holiday brothers, Justin, Jrue, and Aaron, represent a unique and inspiring story of sibling success and resilience in the NBA.

Their dedication, talent, and the bond they share over their love for basketball have propelled them to make history and leave an indelible mark on the sport. 

The Holiday brothers' journey is a testament to the enduring power of family, unity, and the shared pursuit of excellence on the basketball court. Their collective presence in the NBA serves as an inspiration and a reminder of the impact that familial bonds and shared passions can have in the world of professional sports.
