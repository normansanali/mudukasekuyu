The top 10 Heaviest UFC Fighters of all time?

Photo By Dave Mandel/Sherdog

The UFC has seen its fair share of heavyweights over the years. These fighters have brought immense power and strength to the octagon, often leaving a trail of destruction in their wake. From knockout artists to grappling specialists, the heaviest UFC fighters have showcased their unique skills and left an indelible mark on the sport. Let's delve into the top 10 heaviest UFC fighters of all time.

1. Tim Sylvia - 6'8", 265 lbs

Tim "The Maine-iac" Sylvia is a former UFC Heavyweight Champion known for his towering height and considerable weight. Standing at 6 feet 8 inches and weighing in at 265 pounds, Sylvia's imposing frame and striking prowess made him a force to be reckoned with in the heavyweight division.

2. Brock Lesnar - 6'3", 265 lbs

Brock Lesnar, a former UFC Heavyweight Champion, brought his formidable size and strength from the world of professional wrestling to the UFC. Standing at 6 feet 3 inches and weighing 265 pounds, Lesnar's explosive athleticism and ground-and-pound style made him a dominant force in the octagon.

3. Stefan Struve - 7'0", 265 lbs

Stefan "Skyscraper" Struve is known for being the tallest fighter in UFC history, standing at an astounding 7 feet tall. Weighing in at 265 pounds, Struve's length and reach provided a unique challenge for his opponents, and his submission skills added another dimension to his game.

4. Shane Carwin - 6'2", 265 lbs

Shane Carwin, a former interim UFC Heavyweight Champion, possessed incredible knockout power and a formidable physique. Standing at 6 feet 2 inches and weighing 265 pounds, Carwin's explosive punching ability and wrestling pedigree made him a feared opponent in the heavyweight division.

5. Alistair Overeem - 6'4", 265 lbs

Alistair Overeem, known for his striking prowess and overall athleticism, has competed in various weight classes throughout his career. Standing at 6 feet 4 inches and weighing 265 pounds during his heavyweight tenure in the UFC, Overeem's combination of striking and clinch work made him a dangerous contender in the division.

6. Mark Hunt - 5'10", 265 lbs

Mark "The Super Samoan" Hunt, despite being relatively shorter than some of his counterparts, packed an incredible amount of power in his punches. Weighing in at 265 pounds, Hunt's raw knockout ability and granite chin made him a fan favorite and a formidable presence in the UFC's heavyweight division.

7. Roy Nelson - 6'0", 263 lbs

Roy "Big Country" Nelson, known for his iconic belly and knockout power, brought a unique blend of skills to the heavyweight division. Standing at 6 feet tall and weighing 263 pounds, Nelson's unorthodox striking and grappling abilities made him a challenging matchup for many heavyweights.

8. Junior dos Santos - 6'4", 250 lbs

Junior "Cigano" dos Santos, a former UFC Heavyweight Champion, possessed exceptional boxing skills and athleticism for a heavyweight. Standing at 6 feet 4 inches and weighing 250 pounds, dos Santos' speed and technical striking made him a standout in the division.

9. Francis Ngannou - 6'4", 263 lbs

Francis "The Predator" Ngannou, known for his bone-crushing power and intimidating presence, has become a force to be reckoned with in the UFC's heavyweight division. Standing at 6 feet 4 inches and weighing 263 pounds, Ngannou's raw athleticism and knockout ability have propelled him to the top of the heavyweight ranks.

10. Ben Rothwell - 6'4", 265 lbs

Ben Rothwell, with his towering presence and submission skills, has been a notable figure in the UFC's heavyweight division. Standing at 6 feet 4 inches and weighing 265 pounds, Rothwell's unorthodox style and grappling acumen have posed a challenge for many of his opponents.

These heavyweights have left an indelible mark on the UFC, showcasing the immense power and skill that define the heavyweight division. Whether through bone-crushing knockouts or technical grappling displays, these fighters have solidified their places as some of the heaviest and most formidable competitors in UFC history.
