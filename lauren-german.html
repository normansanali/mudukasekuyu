Lauren German- Biography

Lauren German, of English and Dutch heritage, was born on November 29, 1978, under the sign of Sagittarius in Huntington Beach, California, USA. She is a 41-year-old actress best known for her roles as Belinda in “A Walk To Remember,” Leslie Shay in “Chicago Fire,” and Chloe Decker in “Lucifer.”

What is the Net Worth of Lauren German? Salary, Earnings

German’s net worth exceeds $1 million as a result of her success as a professional actor in the film industry since 2000, with appearances in over 30 TV and film titles.

Lauren German- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

Lauren German grew up in Huntington Beach, California, where she was reared by her parents; her father is a vascular surgeon. Her nationality is American, and she is of White Caucasian descent. Lauren graduated from Los Alamitos High School. She even went to Orange County High School of the Arts to study dancing. She enrolled at the University of Southern California to study Anthropology after matriculation.

Lauren German- Relationship, Boyfriend

Lauren German was romantically involved with Barry Zito, a former professional baseball player, and musician. Between 2009 and 2010, she was also romantically involved with actor Ashton Holmes. There were also reports that she was dating actor Tom Ellis, but this was never confirmed. She is now unmarried, has never married, and has no children. Lesley-Ann Brandt, an actress, is one of her closest friends.

Lauren German- Professional Career

Lauren German began her acting career on stage when she first appeared in productions of “Oliver” and “Peter Pan.” In the year 2000, she had her television debut as Kimmy in an episode of the MTV anthology series “Undressed,” followed by her film debut as a lovestruck woman in the romantic comedy-drama film “Down To You,” alongside Freddie Prinze Jr. and Julia Stiles. She appeared as Marie in the episode “Apologize” of The WB’s family drama series “7th Heaven” in 2001, and then as Beth in the made-for-TV film “Shotgun Love Dolls” in 2002.

Ascension to prominence

Lauren rose to notoriety in 2002, when she appeared in Adam Shankman’s coming-of-age romantic tragedy film “A Walk To Remember,” as Belinda, the ex-girlfriend of Landon Carter, played by Shane West, in the film directed by Adam Shankman and based on Nicholas Sparks’ novel of the same name. She then played Darcy Peters in the horror film “Dead Above Ground,” followed by Elena in the drama “A Midsummer Night’s Rave,” both in 2002.
Lauren appeared as Emily Landry in the made-for-TV western action picture “The Lone Ranger” the next year, after which she portrayed a suicidal hitchhiker in Marcus Nispel’s 2003 horror thriller “The Texas Chainsaw Massacre,” after losing the lead role to Jessica Biel.

Success in the Future

Lauren was cast as Rose Miller in the UPN soap opera “Sex, Love & Secrets” in 2005; however, the show was canceled after one season. During the same year, she appeared as Melissa in the thriller “Rx,” Jennifer in the romantic comedy “Standing Still,” and Gertle in the criminal drama “Born Killers.”

Her second job occurred the following year when she played Maddy in the made-for-TV film “Surrender, Dorothy,” followed by roles as Mary in Elizabeth Harrison’s 2007 romantic drama “Love And Mary” and Beth in Eli Roth’s 2007 horror “Hostel: Part II.” Lauren was cast as Gina in the mystery thriller “Dark Country,” directed by Thomas Jane, two years later. In 2010, she was cast as Henley Boone / Chloe in the ABC drama series “Happy Town.”

“Hawaii Five-0,” “Chicago Fire,” and “Lucifer” have all found success on television.
Lauren German played Eva in Xavier Gens’ sci-fi horror film “The Divide” in 2011. She then appeared as Angie Anderson in an episode of Fox’s action-drama series “Human Target” and as Kaylee Slater in two episodes of TNT’s drama series “Memphis Beat.” Following that, she was cast as DHS agent Lauren ‘Lori’ Weston in the second season of the CBS action police procedural series “Hawaii Five-0” (2011-2012).

German next played Leslie Shay in the first iteration of the “Chicago” franchise, “Chicago Fire,” from 2012 to 2015; she also appeared in two episodes of “Chicago P.D.” in 2014. Lauren began filming the Fox urban fantasy series “Lucifer” in 2016, following the final season of “Chicago Fire,” and has been starring in the principal role of Det. Chloe Decker ever since.
