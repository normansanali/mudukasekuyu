Tof Henry, whose real name is Christophe “Tof” Henry, was a French freeskier from Chamonix who rose to legendary fame.

His daring, lightning-fast descents down steep, mountaineer-access courses elevated him to a legendary status in Chamonix and served as an inspiration to skiers all over the globe.

The skier had an incredible career that brought him great renown and a large following.

Regretfully, the legend has passed away. Let’s investigate the circumstances surrounding this legendary skier’s death in more detail.

Cause Of The Tof Henry Accident And Death

Tof Henry passed away on October 11, 2023, while on an excursion atop the Putiagudo Volcano in the Los Lagos area of Chile.

Juan Señoret, a mountain guide from Chile, also lost his life in the unfortunate tragedy. When Tof Henry and Juan were skiing down from the 2439-meter top, they stumbled into a steep area.

Additionally, the expedition’s photographer, Mathurin Vauthier, miraculously avoided harm. This horrifying accident’s exact cause is still unknown.

Obituary of Tof Henry: Honoring the Brave Pioneer

Tof Henry, a native of Chamonix, became renowned for his daring descents on some of the steepest and most significant routes in the area.

After switching from racing to large mountain skiing, he accomplished an incredible accomplishment in 2018 when he and fellow Frenchman Jonathan Charlet became the first people to ski up and down the North Face of Aiguille de Triolet.

The late Andreas Fransson conferred upon him the title of “fastest skier in Chamonix,” recognizing his persistent dedication to pushing the limits of skiing.

The outdoor sports community, fans, and other athletes expressed their sorrow and sympathies upon learning of Henry’s premature passing.
