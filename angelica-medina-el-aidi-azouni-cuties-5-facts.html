Angelica Actress Medina El Aidi-Azouni From Netflix Movie Cuties

Medina El Aidi-Azouni is one of four actresses selected to play the leading characters in the French movie Cuties / Mignonnes. Her character in the film is Angelica, and the film is set for a release on Netflix this September.

The movie is coming to the small screen worldwide amid some controversy about the material being dealt with in the film. Parental associations worldwide were working hard to cancel the movie due to the film's content.

Still, Netflix is standing firm in its decision to release the debut film of Medina El Aidi-Azouni. So, here are five quick facts you need to know about the actress.

Medina El Aidi-Azouni Plays Angelica in the Netflix Movie Cuties / Mignonnes

Cuties is a new film from France, directed by Maimouna Doucoure, her debut film. The movie made rounds of the film festivals where the director was awarded for her work at Sundance. Netflix picked up the international distribution rights for the film and is releasing it in September.

The new Netflix film follows Amy, an eleven-year-old Muslim girl, who lives with her mother in the poor part of Paris. She is raised by her aunt to be a good girl, wife, and mother in the future, but when she comes in contact with other girls in the neighborhood, Amy decides to join their twerking dance crew trying to make it big on the internet.

Medina El Aidi-Azouni plays Angelica in Cuties / Mignonnes. Angelica is one of the group's leading members who welcome Amy into the group when she first tries to join them. Angelica and the other three girls come together to get their social status and compete in local dance competitions.

Medina El Aidi-Azouni Movie Cuties Caused a Lot of Controversies

Netflix released the trailer, poster, and synopsis of Cuties, and some people started to see problems with the way the movie was described. The movie was labeled child-abusing content with people swarming the trailer with dislikes and negatively reviewing the film on Google before it is even released.

There were some concerning things about the film, but Netflix was at fault with their marketing using provocative pictures and sensual language in the promotion. The movie itself is intended to be a criticism of how kids are sexualized in the current world where social media has taken hold.

Going for likes and only through likes, little girls are building their self-esteem, which is problematic. These are the things discussed in the movie, but the marketing made it seem like the film was exploiting kids for entertainment purposes.

Due to the outrage caused on the internet, Netflix changed the marketing materials and also apologized public. But due to the concerns of the people, some even went as far as to threaten the maker of the movie with bodily harm.

The petition to cancel the movie and dropping it from Netflix has over 140,000 signatures as of this writing. There was some miscommunication, which resulted in the film getting negative attention, but the director of Cuties is hopeful that people will see the movie for what it is.

Beating the Field – She is Young and Talented

When it comes to casting young people in a movie, casting directors like to play the field. This way, they have a better chance of finding actors who will not buckle under pressure and can perform at a high level while also going to school.

For the casting process of Cuties / Mignonnes, the team looked over 600 kids, and at the time of casting, Medina El Aidi-Azouni was only nine years old. She won the role and is the youngest of the four leads in the movie. She is a young talent with a smart head on her shoulders.
